Belly Button Biodiversity Dashboard

Use the interactive charts below to explore the dataset


Using Plotly, JavaScript, CSS and HTML, the visualizations below show the results of a study of bacterial species derived from sampling the bacterial species present in ellybuttons. The purpose of the research study is to determine if any of the cultures could be used to synthesize proteins for artificial beef

Demographic Info


The Demographic Info panel above shows information about each participant.


The bar graph above displays the top 10 bacterial cultures that were found in the bellybuttons of each participant. When the mouse hovers over a bar, the number of cultures found in the participant's bellybutton and the name of the bacteria are displayed.


The gauge graph above gives a count of how many times the particpant washed their bellybutton per week.


The bubble chart above displays the details of the bacteria that were found in the participant's bellybutton. The circles are larger if the number of bacterial cultures were were greater. The colors of the circles also are dependent upon the number of bacterial cultures: yellow circles show the greatest concentration of a bacterial culture, green circles are a lower freqency, then blue, and then purple. The name of the bacterial culture is displayed when the mouse hovers over the circle.
